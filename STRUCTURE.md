# Gurufin GitBook Structure Documentation

## Overview

This GitBook documentation is structured to provide a comprehensive guide to the Gurufin ecosystem, covering everything from high-level concepts to detailed technical specifications and developer resources.

## Documentation Structure

### 1. Introduction (3 pages)
- **Welcome to Gurufin**: Overview of the ecosystem and its vision
- **Why Build on Gurufin?**: Key advantages and differentiators
- **Core Principles**: Design philosophy and guiding principles

### 2. Core Concepts (4 pages)
- **Hybrid Execution Model**: Dual AMM + RFQ system
- **Guru-PEG**: Fiat-predictable transaction fees
- **Sovereign Stablecoin Network**: GX Stablecoin model
- **Payment-vs-Payment Settlement**: Atomic cross-chain settlement

### 3. Gurufin Chain (4 pages)
- **Architecture**: Technical architecture and consensus
- **Interoperability**: IBC, EVM Gateway, and cross-chain connectivity
- **Tokenomics (GXN)**: Native token utility and economics
- **Governance**: On-chain governance and validator roles

### 4. GX Stablecoins (4 pages)
- **Overview**: Introduction to GX Stablecoins (USGX, KRGX, JPGX, PHGX)
- **Reserve & Backing**: 1:1 fiat backing and proof-of-reserves
- **Mint & Burn Mechanism**: Automated bank-API-anchored process
- **Compliance & Regulation**: Jurisdictional PoA and embedded compliance

### 5. GuruDex (4 pages)
- **Architecture**: FXSwapMaster, HybridStablePool, PriceOracle
- **Hybrid Pools**: Combined institutional and retail liquidity
- **Dual Swap Algorithms**: AMM for retail, oracle pricing for institutions
- **Liquidity & Rewards**: LP mechanics and reward distribution

### 6. Developer Resources (2 pages)
- **Get Started**: Quick-start guide for developers
- **Connect to Gurufin**: Network connection instructions

### 7. Use Cases (4 pages)
- **Cross-Border Payments**: Remittance and B2B payment solutions
- **Stablecoin FX Trading**: Trading, arbitrage, and derivatives
- **Institutional DeFi**: Tokenized assets and collateral management
- **Government & CBDC**: Central bank and FMI applications

## Content Approach

The documentation follows best practices observed from leading blockchain projects:

1. **Progressive Disclosure**: Content flows from basic concepts to advanced technical details
2. **Use-Case Driven**: Emphasizes what users can build and achieve
3. **Professional Writing**: Uses complete paragraphs and tables instead of excessive bullet points
4. **Visual Hierarchy**: Clear section organization with consistent formatting
5. **Practical Focus**: Includes concrete examples and actionable guidance

## Navigation Design

The SUMMARY.md file provides a clear table of contents with logical grouping of related topics. Each section builds upon previous knowledge, allowing readers to either follow a linear path or jump to specific topics of interest.
