# Governance (Delegators)

The "06 Governance (Delegators)" module details the role and participation of **Delegators** within the Gurufin Chain's decentralized governance framework. It highlights how delegators contribute to decision-making and protocol evolution through the Delegated Proof-of-Stake (DPoS) consensus mechanism.

## Overview

Gurufin Chain leverages an on-chain governance system that necessitates the collective participation of both **Validators** and **Delegators**. This system ensures decentralized decision-making by allowing token holders to influence various aspects of the protocol.

## Key Concepts

### Delegators

**Delegators** are token holders who participate in the network's governance indirectly. Instead of running their own validator nodes, they delegate their staked GRFN tokens to trusted **Validators**. By doing so, delegators confer their voting power to the chosen validator, enabling that validator to represent their interests in governance proposals. In return for delegating, delegators typically share in both the rewards generated by the validator (e.g., from block production) and the responsibilities associated with their validator's actions.

### Relationship with Validators

The relationship between **Delegators** and **Validators** is symbiotic:
*   **Delegators** elect **Validators** by allocating their stake, thereby determining which nodes produce blocks and hold significant voting power.
*   **Validators** act as representatives for their delegated stake in governance votes and are responsible for maintaining network security and functionality.

## Governance Process and Delegator Influence

While **Delegators** do not directly submit or vote on proposals, their choice of **Validator** is crucial. Their delegated stake contributes to the total bonded voting power of a validator, directly impacting the validator's influence on the outcome of proposals.

The governance system handles various **Proposal Types**, including:
*   **Parameter Changes**: Adjustments to network parameters like transaction fees, staking requirements, or consensus configurations.
*   **Protocol Upgrades**: Implementation of new features, additions of new modules, or security patches to the core protocol.
*   **Treasury Allocation**: Decisions regarding the use of community funds for development grants, marketing initiatives, or other community incentives.

All proposals undergo a structured lifecycle:
1.  **Submission**: Proposals require a deposit to be submitted.
2.  **Voting Period**: A standard 7-day voting window where validators (representing their delegators) can vote "Yes," "No," "Abstain," or "No with Veto."
3.  **Timelock & Execution**: Approved proposals enter a 72-hour timelock before automatic execution on-chain, ensuring transparency and providing a buffer period.

For **Parameter Changes**, a specific quorum requirement of 10–20% of bonded voting power is in place, demonstrating the importance of widespread participation (indirectly via delegators' stake) in critical network adjustments.
